Religious and Biker Species

One Species of Religion

Brahma Sarovar, Kurukshetra

Mythology: According to Hindu epics (Itihasa), Lord Brahma created the universe after performing a grand yajna (ritual sacrifice) on the very land of Kurukshetra. Brahma Sarovar is thus believed to be the cradle of civilization itself.

Gateway of India, Mumbai

Commemoration: The monument was erected to honor the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary, the first British monarch and his consort to visit India, in December 1911.

India Gate, New Delhi

Standing tall in Delhi, the India Gate is a majestic war memorial. Built under British rule, it commemorates the sacrifices of Indian soldiers. The Imperial War Graves Commission, established after World War I, commissioned the monument. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, a renowned architect, the foundation stone was laid in 1921.

Golden Temple, Amritsar

Architect and Significance: While the exact architect isn't documented, tradition suggests Hazrat Mian Mir, a Muslim saint, laid the foundation stone. This collaboration highlights the spirit of religious tolerance embedded in Sikhism. Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Guru, is credited with constructing the original temple structure in 1588 or 1604.
